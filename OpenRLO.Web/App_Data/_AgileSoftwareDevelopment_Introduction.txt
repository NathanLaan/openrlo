The [Agile Manifesto][1] defines agile software development as being a series of [4 core values][1], and [12 guiding principles][3] whose purpose is to provide a better way of developing software.

Agile software development is a broad term that encompasses a number of different, but similar, methodologies, such as SCRUM, Extreme Programming, and the Adaptive Software Development process ([Larman, 2004][4]).
