The [Agile Manifesto](http://agilemanifesto.org/) defines agile software development as being a series of 4 core values, and 12 guiding principles whose purpose is to provide a better way of developing software.

Agile software development is a broad term that encompasses a number of different, but similar, methodologies, such as SCRUM, Extreme Programming, and the Adaptive Software Development process (Larman, Craig (2004). Agile and Iterative Development: A Manager's Guide. Addison-Wesley. p. 27. ISBN 978-0-13-111155-4).
